For Your Changing Needs

A woman needs an additional 300 calories a day when she’s pregnant. This is why it’s important to consume food that offers nutrition, and not just plain, empty calories.

Research shows that 1 in every 4 pregnant Filipinas is nutritionally at-risk. DOH has identified the most common nutrient deficiencies among pregnant women to be the following:

Caloric energy – intake was found to be only 78.4% adequate
Iron – was only 28.8% adequate
Calcium – only 12.4% met the estimated average requirement
Folic Acid – 37.5% of pregnant women are deficient

These deficiencies affect both you and your baby’s health. As the baby grows in your tummy, so does your hunger and need for more nutrients.
